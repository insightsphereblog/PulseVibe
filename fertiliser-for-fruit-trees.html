Fertiliser for Fruit Trees: The Key to Healthier Trees and Sweeter Harvests

By PulseVibe Team | October 20, 2025

Fruit trees are among the most rewarding plants to grow—providing beauty, shade, and delicious harvests season after season. But to perform at their best, these trees require more than sunshine and water. The right fertiliser for fruit trees plays a vital role in promoting healthy roots, abundant blossoms, and sweet, full-flavoured fruit.

While general-purpose fertilisers might support basic growth, they often lack the specific nutrient balance required by fruiting trees. That’s why targeted products like CompleteGrow’s Advanced NPK 4‑18‑38 + Calcium & Magnesium are emerging as game-changers for both backyard growers and commercial orchards across Australia.

Why Fruit Trees Need Specialised Fertiliser

Unlike ornamental trees or lawn grasses, fruit trees undergo a demanding growth cycle that includes root development, flowering, fruit set, and ripening. Each stage demands a specific nutrient focus:

  • Early growth and root development need phosphorus.
  • Flowering and fruit setting rely on both phosphorus and potassium.
  • Fruit filling and ripening depend heavily on potassium, with calcium and magnesium playing support roles.
  • Nitrogen, while necessary, should be carefully managed to avoid excessive leafy growth at the expense of fruit.

Using a fertiliser that’s not tailored for fruit trees can lead to poor flowering, low yields, and bland or small fruit. For consistent performance, the nutrient input must match the tree’s seasonal needs.

The Ideal Nutrient Profile: NPK 4‑18‑38 + Ca & Mg

CompleteGrow’s fertiliser solution has been engineered specifically for fruit trees, with a nutrient profile that supports their full growth and production cycle.

Balanced NPK Ratio

  • 4% Nitrogen (N): Just enough to maintain leaf health without overpromoting vegetative growth.
  • 18% Phosphorus (P): Excellent for stimulating root development and supporting robust flower production.
  • 38% Potassium (K): Critical for fruit size, colour, sweetness, and stress resistance.

This formula prioritises reproductive growth (flowers and fruit) over excessive foliage—perfect for trees that are already established and heading into their productive years.

Calcium and Magnesium

  • Calcium strengthens cell walls, reduces fruit cracking, and improves storage life.
  • Magnesium supports chlorophyll production and overall energy metabolism in the plant.

These secondary nutrients are often overlooked in standard fertilisers but are vital for fruit quality and tree health—especially under the challenging conditions of Australian climates.

Benefits of Using the Right Fertiliser for Fruit Trees

Increased Fruit Yield: The right nutrient mix results in more blossoms, better pollination, and a higher fruit set rate. This leads directly to more fruit per tree.

Sweeter, Juicier Fruit: High potassium levels directly influence sugar production and flavour development. You’ll notice a significant difference in the taste and size of your fruit when potassium levels are optimised.

Stronger, Healthier Trees: Adequate phosphorus and calcium help build a robust root system and stronger branches, supporting better nutrient uptake and withstanding weather stresses.

Improved Resistance to Pests and Diseases: A well-nourished tree is naturally more resilient. Balanced nutrients help maintain thick leaves, firm fruit, and better immunity against common pests and diseases.

Better Storage and Shelf Life: Calcium improves fruit firmness, which means longer-lasting, less perishable fruit—ideal for both fresh consumption and longer-term storage.

Tailored for Australian Conditions

One of the standout features of this fertiliser is its formulation for Australian conditions. Whether you’re growing citrus in Queensland, stone fruit in Victoria, or apples in Tasmania, local soil types and climate variations can impact nutrient availability. CompleteGrow’s product is formulated to meet these specific challenges, ensuring your trees receive what they need—regardless of your location.

Many Australian soils are sandy, acidic, or low in organic matter. This can reduce the availability of essential nutrients like potassium and magnesium. By choosing a fertiliser that addresses these gaps, you set your trees up for long-term productivity.

Application Tips for Best Results

To get the most from a specialised fertiliser like this, proper application is key:

  • Timing: Start feeding in early spring, just before flowering begins. Continue during fruit development for best results. Avoid late-season nitrogen applications to prevent off-season growth.
  • Frequency: Apply every 2–4 weeks during active growth, or as recommended. Liquid formulations are ideal for regular feeding.
  • Watering: Always apply to moist soil and water in thoroughly to prevent root burn and ensure even nutrient distribution.
  • Coverage: Apply around the drip line of the tree, where feeder roots are most active—not just at the base of the trunk.
  • Monitor Response: Look for improved flowering, richer green leaves, and more uniform fruit as signs the fertiliser is working.
